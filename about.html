Our Work

Details about the work done by Uganda Development and Health Associates, including different projects and initiatives.

Introduction

Uganda Development and Health Associates (UDHA) started its operations as a Community Based Organization (CBO) in April of 2003 in the Iganga District. The organization has been involved in various community-based activities ever since, specializing in HIV/AIDS prevention, Malaria control, sanitation, adolescent sexual and reproductive health, and school health and immunization.

In August of 2005, UDHA expanded its operations to the Mayuge and Bugiri Districts in a new capacity as a Non-Government Organization (NGO).

The organization attracted a significant number of professionals in health, social work, law, agriculture, community development, education, and environment. These actions were appreciated as UDHA recognized that community problems are multi-causal and therefore require multi-sectoral approaches with strong community participation. UDHA improves access to services dedicated to enhancing and expanding healthcare for underserved communities. This often includes empowering young mothers and children using sustainable mechanisms. Since 2003, UDHA has become a regional role model in health promotion and has been even more committed to serving the poor and vulnerable in Uganda.

Empowerment of Communities

Goal: To empower communities with a focus on health and advocacy through a multi-sectoral approach to community development.

Focus: Families at a household level

Resilient Households

Goal: Vulnerable and marginalized people are economically empowered and have resilient livelihoods.

Focus: Sustainable access and control over natural and productive resources, gender-sensitive agriculture and the value chains, youth skilling, resilience and adaptation to climate change, and business development.

Community Participation

Goal: To promote community participatory approaches that enhance the health and development of the most vulnerable members of the community.

Focus: Children, youths out of school, orphans and widows, and the most-at-risk populations.

To contribute to change that lasts, we mobilize to transform the systems, policies, and practices that have the most significant impact on people's lives.
